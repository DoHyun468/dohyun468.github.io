About me

I like turning cameras into measuring instruments.

I'm an engineer and researcher working on 3D perception and AI research. I studied mechanical and electrical engineering at KAIST, where I measured ship-scale structures with calibrated stereo cameras. These days at Recon Labs I rebuild real scenes as neural representations and build AI agents that help people create.

What connects all of it: taking a picture, and ending up with something you can trust about the world — a coordinate, a shape, a decision.

— focus

Three things I keep coming back to

Camera geometry

how pixels become coordinates

Calibration, stereo matching, multi-view geometry, LiDAR. The quiet math that decides whether a measurement can be trusted.

Neural scenes

how photos become places

3D Gaussian Splatting and NeRF — reconstruction quality for real-world captures: artifacts, backgrounds, regularization, and what breaks when the input is imperfect.

AI agents

how models become coworkers

Agent harnesses and tool-use workflows — systems where a model understands what you're working on and helps you take the next step.
